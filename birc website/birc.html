Present Leadership

The present leadership of the Bonny Integrated Recruitment centre(B.I.R.C) is comprised of youthful, innovative and visionary leader as lead by MAYOR ALADIOKUMA HART. Since his inception, he initiated a full digitalization of the bureau. The setup of the database system and modernise website is a testament of a progressive vision; one which seeks to use top-notch technologies to serve the Bonny Kingdom better. Furthermore, there is a functional pre-induction centre: for training; computerize offices, an ICT centre, a revamped monitoring team office and much more. Beside the obvious hundereds of applicants that have gotten job under this leadership; there is an on-going training for different computer packages, instrumentation-just concluded- geared towards improving the skill-sets of the native people. These are self-evident testaments to a result-oriented approach of the journey thus far.

About Us

The Bonny integrated Recruitment Centre, located in Bonny Island: is the leading recruitment agency in Nigeria serving companies both local and multi-national. It has a unifying vision for adequate employment for both indigenes and non-indigenes in and out of Bonny Island. The Centre wants a vibrant local and global economy where employers can find the skills and aptitudes needed.
Our vision is complementary to the bonny island / local government area vision in terms of job creation and empowerment and the new economic strategy for the island a step change for Bonny. The BIRC strategy will entrench the rivers state executives workforce plan and align with the new strategy.

Our Vision, Our Mission

Upon his enthronement; the king his Majesty Edward Asimini Dappa William Pepple lll Perekule Xl Jp Con, Amanyanabo and Natural Ruler Of Grand Bonny saw the desparity in the employment of the Bonny youths. As a pro-active King and Leader, his Majesty created the Bonny Kingdom Employment Bureau; now the Bonny Integrated Recruitment Centre among other committees, to bridge this gap. And to ensure that the Bonny Youths are gainfully employed.
His Majesty magnammously ensured that non-indigenes also benefits from this institution, through the creation of the non-indigenes office occupied by natives from different tribes and regions of Nigeria. Since the establishment of BIRC, His Majesty has given this institution the mandate to interface and mediate on issues of employment with the companies operating in Bonny.

Birc wants to alleviate the endermic rise of unemployment of recent years. However, progress now requires more than existing interventions can offer. The centre leadership headed by Mayor Aladiokuma Hart wants to achieve a step change in employment and skills development. This will entail strengthened local leadership, effective employer engagement, collaborative, inter-agency planning and an integrated delivery system which provides a whole needs approach to the issues and barriers experienced by workless and low skilled individuals. The centre believes that this BIRC strategy will be a prime catalyst and vehicle for this to be achieved.
Over the BIRC strategy two-year period we will demonstrate that we have created an approach and structure that shows maximum capability in terms of co-ordinating and aligning multi-agency activity in order to improve efficiency and effectiveness. Taking into account the size of the gap between the current employment rate and the target, restricting our planning to two years will see us achieve a modest impact, but participation in this endeavour is the start of a long term commitment to support and drive institutional and cultural change, to substantially and sustainably increase employment and skills levels in bonny.
